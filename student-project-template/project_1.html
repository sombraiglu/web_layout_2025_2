Personal Notes

What fits my workflow: Bootstrap and HTML5 UP are great when I just want to get a page looking decent fast — no fuss, quick results. Tailwind and Flowbite are awesome when I want full control over design and keep everything looking clean and consistent. A bit more setup, but worth it. MUI is perfect for React projects or dashboards — everything looks polished without much styling work. Anime.js is super fun for adding motion and little animations that make the site feel alive. Hygraph and ImageKit.io handle content and media like pros — flexible, fast, and keeps my projects organized. Trade-offs I noticed: Speed vs. Flexibility: Bootstrap/HTML5 UP = fast, simple. Tailwind/Flowbite = slower to set up, but you can make it totally your own. Dependencies vs. Simplicity: Some tools need Node.js and extra setup (Tailwind, Flowbite, MUI), while Bootstrap and HTML5 UP just work in the browser. Creativity vs. Convention: Anime.js and Tailwind let me get creative. Bootstrap and MUI steer you toward safe, standard designs.

How I’d use them in my portfolio: I’d mix and match: Bootstrap or HTML5 UP for quick pages, Tailwind + Flowbite for polished, custom sections, MUI for app-like projects, Anime.js for subtle animations, and Hygraph + ImageKit.io to keep content and images organized and fast-loading. That way, my portfolio looks good, works smoothly, and is easy to update.
